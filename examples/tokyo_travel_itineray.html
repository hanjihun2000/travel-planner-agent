Day 2: Culture in Asakusa & Trendy Harajuku

  • Morning: Head to Asakusa and visit Senso-ji Temple, Tokyo's oldest temple. Explore Nakamise-dori market for souvenirs and street food.
  • Lunch: Try tempura in Asakusa.
  • Afternoon: Take the subway to Harajuku. Stroll down Takeshita Street for unique fashion and quirky snacks.
  • Evening: Visit the Meiji Jingu Shrine, a peaceful oasis dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken.
  • Dinner: Explore the diverse dining options in Harajuku.

Day 3: Shibuya Crossing & Ginza Elegance

  • Morning: Experience the iconic Shibuya Crossing, the world's busiest intersection. Visit the Hachiko statue.
  • Lunch: Enjoy ramen or sushi in Shibuya.
  • Afternoon: Explore Ginza, Tokyo's upscale shopping district. Window shop at high-end boutiques or visit the Kabuki-za Theatre.
  • Dinner: Indulge in a slightly more upscale dinner in Ginza (look for set menus to save money).

Important Notes

  • Purchase a Japan Rail Pass if you plan on doing a lot of long-distance train travel (e.g., day trip to Hakone). Otherwise, a Suica or Pasmo card is sufficient for Tokyo's subway system.
  • Learn basic Japanese phrases. While many people in tourist areas speak English, knowing some Japanese will enhance your experience.
  • Carry cash. While credit cards are becoming more widely accepted, many smaller establishments still prefer cash.

Booking Requirements

  • Flights: Book as soon as possible to secure the best prices and outbound flight CX 526.
  • Accommodation: Reserve your hotel (APA Hotel Nishi Shinjuku Gochome Eki Tower) immediately.
  • Narita Express: Book tickets in advance online to avoid queues.
  • Hakone Day Trip: If you plan to visit Hakone, consider booking transportation and activities in advance, especially during weekends and holidays.

Local Tips

  • Explore convenience stores (like 7-Eleven and Lawson) for affordable and delicious snacks and meals.
  • Take advantage of free activities like visiting parks, temples, and the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building.
  • Eat like a local! Try ramen, sushi, tempura, yakitori, and other Japanese specialties.
  • Be aware of train rush hour (typically 7:00-9:00 AM and 5:00-7:00 PM).
